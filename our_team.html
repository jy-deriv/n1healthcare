Clinicians

Professor Lakshminarayan Ranganath

Metabolic medicine

Professor Lakshminarayan Ranganath, a consultant at the Royal Liverpool University Hospital, UK, is a prominent figure in metabolic medicine. He founded the NHS-funded National Alkaptonuria Centre (NAC) and serves as its inaugural Director. Prof Ranganath's notable achievements include conducting a national survey identifying AKU patients globally, pioneering AKU patient assessments, and leading DevelopAKUre, an EU-funded program involving a major nitisinone clinical trial. He has authored over 200 peer-reviewed papers and mentored numerous research students.

Dr. Glenn H. Bock

Pediatrics and Nephrology

Dr. Bock worked as an academic clinician and physician-scientist for nearly 40 years. He offers a great deal of knowledge that includes both hereditary and acquired kidney diseases, transplantation medicine, renal replacement therapies and a broad understanding of immunology, autoimmune diseases, nutrition, longevity, and gene therapies. He has authored more than 40 peer-reviewed papers and has presented his research at national and international scientific symposia. He has mentored numerous subspecialty fellows, many of whom hold senior leadership positions at well-regarded institutions throughout the US.
